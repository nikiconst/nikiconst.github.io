Analysis of tourism arrivals in Cyprus

The aim of of the project

Tourism is one of the most critical sectors of the Cyprus economy, contributing substantially both directly and indirectly to the country’s GDP. The aim of the project is to analyse in more detail the Cyprus tourist sector through an examination of its contribution in the country’s GDP as well as examination of relevant statistics for tourist arrivals in an attempt to identify changes and trends over the past 10 years. The country of origin of tourists visiting Cyprus as well as the revenue per visitor is also examined with the aim of identifying the most critical countries from where tourists come from as well as changes in the average spending per tourists.

The number of tourist arrivals in Cyprus has a direct positive effect on Cyprus’ GDP growth. It can be observed from the graph below that when the number of tourists visiting Cyprus increases, its GDP rises as well and vice versa. This is because tourism affects a variety of different sectors. For example, it can be included in sectors such as “Accommodation & food service”, “Electricity & Water supply”, “Wholesale & retail trade” and “Transportation & storage”. Specifically, according to the “World Data Atlas” tourism contributes for about 23% of the whole country’s GDP.
