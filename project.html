Analysis of tourism arrivals in Cyprus


The aim of the project
Tourism is one of the most critical sectors of the Cyprus economy, contributing substantially both directly and indirectly to the country’s GDP. The aim of the project is to analyse in more detail the Cyprus tourist sector through an examination of its contribution in the country’s GDP as well as examination of relevant statistics for tourist arrivals in an attempt to identify changes and trends over the past 10 years. The country of origin of tourists visiting Cyprus as well as the revenue per visitor is also examined with the aim of identifying the most critical countries from where tourists come from as well as changes in the average spending per tourists.

The number of tourist arrivals in Cyprus has a direct positive effect on Cyprus’ GDP growth. It can be observed from the graph below that when the number of tourists visiting Cyprus increases, its GDP rises as well and vice versa. This is because tourism affects a variety of different sectors. For example, it can be included in sectors such as “Accommodation & food service”, “Electricity & Water supply”, “Wholesale & retail trade” and “Transportation & storage”. Specifically, according to the “World Data Atlas” tourism contributes for about 23% of the whole country’s GDP.

Tourists in Cyprus come from many different countries, while Europe being the main source of tourists. From the map above it is observed that the majority of visitors come from the UK (1,330,635 visitors in 2019), followed by Russian visitors (781,856 visitors in 2019).
As the UK historically appears to be the most critical market for Cyprus tourism there is further analysis to consider whether the UK economic conditions (such as GDP growth and the exchange rate) could be one of the reasons for changes in UK tourist arrivals in Cyprus.

Figure 6: UK 's GDP growth in comparison with the number of UK citizens visiting Cyprus

Figure 7: The Euro to Pound exchange rate over time


As shown from the graph above it is observed that UK’s GDP affects the number of UK tourists in Cyprus when there are significant changes in the GDP, such as the 4.51% decrease of UK’s GDP occurred in 2009. Such a fall resulted in a 13.96% decrease of UK tourist arrivals in Cyprus. However, following the year 2009, despite the fact that GDP remained stable over the years, the number of tourist arrivals fluctuated. This seems that the Euro:Pound exchange rate may have a more significant effect on the number of UK tourist arrivals in Cyprus. During the year 2015 onwards, GDP seemed to remain stable at a rate of 2%, whereas, there seems to be a significant appreciation of the pound and an increase of UK visitors in Cyprus.
Conclusion:
In conclusion, it can be realized that tourism plays a major role in Cyprus’ economy in terms of its GDP including the income received. However, it is realized that other country’s economies have a significant effect on Cyprus’ economy since it affects the number of citizens leaving their country of origin in order to visit Cyprus.
